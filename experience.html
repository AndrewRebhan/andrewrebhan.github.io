Experience

Mehlville School District (Teacher)

  • Taught English Language Arts content to 7th grade students
  • Made use of technology to facilitate project-based learning in various forms such as podcasts, videos, shared documents, screenplays, and more
  • Assumed leadership positions through the PBIS committee, creating a digital student behavior/intervention tracker, and presenting teaching methods to peers

Teaching English Language Arts consists of a 90-minute block per class. 45 minutes are for reading instruction, and another 45 for writing instruction. I used spreadsheets to track reading and writing and help my students set goals for themselves. I am proud to say that my students have read over 100,000 pages this last year and collectively wrote nearly half a million words in one month while participating in National Novel Writing Month

Mehlville School District (Substitute Teaching)

  • Instructed students age 5-18 through substitute teaching
  • Built relationships with students from various schools and classroom climates
  • Frequently requested by teachers to return to their classrooms

Substitute teaching helped me hone my classroom management skills across a wide range of age groups. Being a substitute teacher before being a full-time teacher also helped me sample a variety of lesson styles before being tasked with creating them for myself.

St. Trinity Summer School (Volunteer Teacher)

  • Ran math instruction for two summers for elementary-aged students from low socio-economic backgrounds
  • Used data to track student growth in math skills of 30+ students

My experience at St. Trinity’s summer school was such a good jumping off point into my teaching career. The students kept me on my toes, and I learned so much about behavior management and instructional design in a matter of weeks. It was here that I learned why Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs is so important. I helped get each student fed and supported, and only then did I focus on instruction.
